# Plan 294 — GOAT Gate G3: Orthogonality to H_1 (MAKE-OR-BREAK)

**Date:** 2026-06-19
**Status:** ✅ PASS — Spearman ρ = 0.0652, 95% CI [-0.017, 0.150], well below 0.5.

## Verdict

**G3 PASS — Super-GOAT PROCEEDS.** On the synthetic-NPC decision suite
(1000 decision points × K=8 trajectories × action_dim=6, same mixture as
G2), the Spearman rank correlation between Shannon entropy H_1(P̄) and
max-trajectory JS-divergence-to-mean `u_max` is **ρ = 0.0652**, with a
bootstrap 95% CI of **[-0.017, 0.150]**. The entire CI is below the 0.5
Super-GOAT threshold.

**Interpretation:** JS-uniqueness captures structurally-different
information from H_1. The two metrics agree on rank-ordering only ~6.5% of
the time (vs 50% expected if they were uncorrelated, vs 100% if they were
monotone functions of each other). The ICT selector's information content
is genuinely orthogonal to the entropy-driven gates we already ship
(`llmexec_guard`, `CuriosityPulse`, `AdaptiveTraceCompactor`, Bebop).

The scatter is bimodal:
- A **near-horizontal band** at u_max ≈ 0.05-0.15 spanning the full H_1
  range — these are "no real branching" cases where no trajectory diverges
  sharply from the mean (typically undecided/noise regime).
- A **vertical column** at H_1 ≈ 1.79 (the uniform-over-6 maximum entropy)
  with varying u_max — these are the noise-regime decision points where the
  population mean is uniform but individual trajectories diverge by varying
  amounts.

The lack of a monotone trend is the orthogonality signal. If H_1 and u_max
carried the same information we'd see a tight positively-sloped cluster.

## Why G3 passes despite G2 borderline-failing

G2 measured the *inflection location* of the within-decision-point
uniqueness curve and found it sits at 37.5% rather than the paper's 10%.
G3 measures a *different property*: the across-decision-points correlation
between H_1 and u_max. These are independent:

- **G2 failing** says "the within-decision-point top-k% inflection doesn't
  sit at 10% for NPCs" — a tuning issue for `k_percent`.
- **G3 passing** says "H_1 and JS-uniqueness are not redundant across
  decision points" — the structural orthogonality the paper claims.

Both can be (and are) true simultaneously. The synthetic suite has the
right *population structure* (varied regimes) for G3 to test orthogonality
even though its *within-decision-point k% inflection* isn't at 10%.

## Implementation notes

- **Spearman ρ via Pearson on ranks:** implemented inline (~30 lines).
  Average-rank tie-breaking. No external dependency.
- **Bootstrap 95% CI:** 1000 resamples with replacement, take 2.5% and
  97.5% quantiles. Deterministic via a seeded LCG.
- **Same synthetic suite as G2** (LCG seed 0x294BEB0B) so the two gates
  are directly comparable.
